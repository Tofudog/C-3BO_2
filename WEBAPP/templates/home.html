What is Leukemia?

Leukemia is a cancer caused by the overproduction of white blood cells, or leukocytes. When those leukocytes' DNA mutates to avoid death, they cause an imbalance against other types of cells: red blood cells and platelets.


It originates in the bone marrow and is classified as a blood cancer.


Among the types of leukemia are

  • Acute Myeloid Leukemia
  • Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia
  • Chronic Myeloid Leukemia
  • Multiple Myeloma
